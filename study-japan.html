Why Study in Japan?

Japan combines academic excellence with cultural richness, providing an ideal environment for international students.

World-Class Education

Japanese universities rank among the world's best, offering cutting-edge research and practical learning.

Work Opportunities

Students can work part-time up to 28 hours per week during studies and full-time during holidays.

Safe & Disciplined Society

Japan consistently ranks as one of the safest countries globally with low crime rates and excellent public safety.

Affordable Education

Japan offers reasonable tuition fees and living costs with numerous scholarship opportunities.

Japanese Education System

Japan offers diverse educational pathways for international students.

Japanese Language Schools

1-2 year intensive Japanese language programs for higher education preparation.

  • Duration: 1-2 years
  • Entry: Basic Japanese (N5)
  • Focus: Language proficiency
  • Pathway: University/College

Specialized Training Colleges

Vocational schools offering practical skills in various professional fields.

  • Duration: 2-3 years
  • Entry: JLPT N2/N3
  • Focus: Professional skills
  • Degree: Advanced Diploma

Universities

Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD programs in various disciplines.

  • Duration: 4 years (Bachelor's)
  • Entry: JLPT N2+ or IELTS
  • Focus: Academic research
  • Degree: Bachelor/Master/PhD

Admission Requirements

To study in Japan, students must meet specific academic, linguistic, and financial requirements.

1

Academic Requirements

  • 12 years of formal education
  • Minimum GPA: 2.5/4.0 or equivalent
  • High school diploma/certificate
  • Academic transcripts (all years)
  • For universities: Entrance exam (EJU)
2

Language Requirements

  • Japanese Language Schools: JLPT N5
  • Specialized Colleges: JLPT N2/N3
  • Universities: JLPT N2 or IELTS 6.0+
  • Certificate of language study (150+ hours)
  • Japanese language proficiency certificate
3

Financial Requirements

  • Proof of financial capability
  • Bank statements (6+ months)
  • Minimum balance: ¥1.5-2 million
  • Sponsor's financial documents
  • Income tax returns (sponsor)

We provide complete guidance on document preparation and verification.

Work & Life in Japan

Japan offers excellent work opportunities and quality of life for international students.

Work Hours

Up to 28 hours/week during semesters, 40 hours/week during vacations.

Salary Range

¥1,000-¥1,500 per hour, earning ¥80,000-¥120,000 monthly.

Job Types

Convenience stores, restaurants, tutoring, hotel staff, delivery services.

Accommodation

Student dormitories, shared apartments, homestays. Rent: ¥25,000-¥50,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about studying, working, and living in Japan.

Is Japanese language mandatory for studying in Japan?

Yes, for most programs Japanese language proficiency is required.

How much can I earn from part-time work in Japan?

Students can earn ¥1,000-¥1,500 per hour, approximately ¥80,000-¥120,000 per month.

What is the total cost of studying in Japan?

Annual costs range from ¥1.3-2.6 million including tuition, accommodation, and living expenses.

Can I work in Japan after graduation?

Yes, Japan allows international students to work after graduation by changing student visa to work visa.

How long does the entire process take?

The complete process takes approximately 6-7 months from language training to departure.
